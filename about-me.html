💐 About Me


Currently...


I'm working as a research assistant for the Columbia University Sslang Project under Professor Stephen Edwards, where I'm programming in Haskell and C to develop the type system of Sslang, a functional language built atop of the Sparse Synchronous Model: a lightweight real-time programming model with deterministic concurrency.

I'm also working as a teaching assistant for COMS 3157 Advanced Programming in C, where I am leading weekly office hours and recitation sessions covering the C programming language, UNIX, Bash, TCP/IP networking, operating systems, and the process of programming a dynamic web server in C for a course with 350+ students.


In my free time...


I enjoy frequenting the New York City café scene (iced oatmilk lattes forever), performing with Sur (Columbia's South Asian a capella group), organizing data science related events as an Executive Board Member for the Columbia Data Science Society, and working on my Barbara Horowitz '55 Scholars of Distinction research project (fully funded by Barnard College) analyzing the 1920s Indo-Fijian labor movement that my ancestors were a part of.
